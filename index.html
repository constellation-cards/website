Flip-a-Card

The Game (Front)

This is a game whose rules are on cards. To add a rule, write a card. Follow every rule on the cards on the table.

    The Game (Back)

    See the other side for rules

      The Facilitator (Front)

      This game has no Facilitator (GM, MC, referee). Resolve player disputes collectively.

        Flip to become the Facilitator
        The Facilitator (Back)

        The game has a Facilitator (GM, MC, referee). Facilitators have final authority over rules questions. Whoever laid this card face up is the facilitator.

          Flip to play cooperatively
          Challenges (Front)

          If any player thinks the outcome of your narration is uncertain, you've been challenged. Meet the challenge by getting a hit via some other rule. If you don't, whoever posed the challenge narrates the outcome instead.

            Flip to resolve via narration
            Challenges (Back)

            Resolve challenges strictly via narration.

              Flip to rely on hits
              Simple Dice (Front)

              The Simple Dice rule is not used in this game

                Flip to use Simple Dice
                Simple Dice (Back)

                If another rule says to meet a challenge, you can roll 1d6:

                • On 1-3, you get nothing
                • On 4-6, you generate 1 hit
                Flip to not use dice

                Condition Cards (Front)

                Cards with this icon indicate an ongoing physical, mental, social, magical, etc. condition for a character.

                • Generate a hit by taking on a new condition
                • Generate a hit by aggravating a condition (flip over)
                • Topside: discard after spending a scene attending to condition
                • Flipside: discard by spending downtime doing so
                Flip to not use Conditions
                Condition Cards (Back)

                Condition cards have no effect

                  Flip to use Conditions
                  Hurt (Front)

                  You've been bloodied, bruised, stunned, or otherwise physically harmed.

                  • Weapon attacks
                  • Creatures' natural attacks
                  • Natural disasters
                  Wounded (Back)

                  Your injuries are severe. You must get medical attention soon.

                  • Significant blood loss
                  • Broken or twisted limbs
                  • Shock or disorientation
                  Weakened (Front)

                  You've exerted yourself, or been mildly hurt, and need time to rest.

                  • Fatigue or exhaustion
                  • Hunger or dehydration
                  • Weather or temperature extremes
                  Ill (Back)

                  You have nothing left to give, and can barely move or act.

                  • Serious exhaustion
                  • Disease or infection
                  • Fever or delirium
                  Obliged (Front)

                  You've incurred a financial or other responsibility.

                  • Creditor asks for favors
                  • You're hassled for repayment
                  • Embarrassment if revealed
                  Indebted (Back)

                  You have significant debts that demand your time or attention.

                  • Pursuit by creditors
                  • Attention from the law
                  • Bad reputation
                  Noticed (Front)

                  Someone's looking for, or at, you.

                  • Scrutiny from city guards
                  • Someone tailing you
                  • Stalked by an animal
                  Pursued (Back)

                  You're being chased, by someone or something that wishes you ill.

                  • Uncomfortable hiding places
                  • Imminent attack
                  • Threat to current goal
